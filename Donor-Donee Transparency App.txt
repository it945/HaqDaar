1. Executive Summary
This document defines the updated MVP for a donor-donee transparency platform. The platform connects donors with manually verified donees. Donees are found and verified outside the app first, then selected donees are uploaded into the app by admin with basic details and receiving QR codes.
The platform is intentionally designed as a no-custody connection and transparency system. It does not receive or hold any donation money. Donors send money directly to the donee, guardian, or approved receiver using EasyPaisa, JazzCash, Raast, bank transfer, or another external payment method.
Main Design Decision
There will be no donor cash wallet. The app may show an Impact Wallet or My Donation Records screen, but it is only a record of pending and verified direct donation proofs. It must never be presented as a spendable cash balance.
2. Non-Custody Declaration
Mandatory Product Statement
The platform will not receive, hold, store, pool, transfer, process, route, or settle any donor money. Donors pay donees or approved receivers directly outside the app. The app only records proof, verification status, and transparency updates.
•	The platform is not a wallet.
•	The platform is not a payment gateway.
•	The platform is not a money transfer service.
•	The platform is not a payment processor, PSO, PSP, EMI, bank, or microfinance institution.
•	The platform does not collect donations into its own account in the MVP.
•	The platform does not settle shopkeepers in the MVP.
•	All donor payments happen outside the app using the donor’s own payment app or bank channel.
3. Updated Donee Discovery Model
Donees will not apply inside the app and will not self-register. Donee discovery and verification will happen offline before a donee is added into the system.
3.1 Offline Donee Identification
•	Your team finds deserving donees outside the app through field work, community references, local shopkeeper references, NGO/social worker references, mosque/community networks, or internal verification visits.
•	The team checks whether the donee is genuinely poor or deserving.
•	The team collects only the minimum basic information needed for donor trust and payment routing.
•	The team confirms whether the receiving QR belongs to the donee, guardian, family member, or another approved receiver.
•	Only selected and approved donees are uploaded into the app by admin.
3.2 No Public Donee Signup
•	There is no donee signup page.
•	There is no donee login.
•	There is no donee mobile app.
•	There is no public request-for-help form in the MVP.
•	A donee cannot add or edit their own information.
•	Only admin can create, edit, approve, pause, or block a donee.
3.3 Basic Details Uploaded by Admin
•	Donee name
•	Guardian name if applicable
•	City and area
•	Need category
•	Reason for support
•	Verification notes
•	Optional photo
•	Receiving payment method
•	Receiving QR image
•	Masked receiving account detail
•	App-generated spending identity QR code
4. Regulatory Orientation
This document is a product and technology planning document, not legal advice. A payment/legal consultant should review the final model before public launch. The low-licensing logic is based on avoiding wallet, stored-value, payment routing, payment processing, and settlement activities.
•	SBP describes Electronic Money Institutions as licensed entities that provide digital payment instruments such as wallets, prepaid cards, and contactless instruments.
•	SBP describes PSO/PSP activity as providing electronic platforms for clearing, processing, routing, and switching electronic transactions.
•	Raast P2M QR is designed for merchants/businesses to accept payments through QR and other channels.
•	The MVP avoids these activities by keeping all payments outside the app and only recording proof and transparency data.
Practical Rule
If the app ever starts receiving funds, pooling funds, creating spendable balances, routing transactions, or settling shopkeepers, the compliance model must be reviewed again before launch.
5. User Roles
5.1 Admin
•	Adds only offline-verified donees into the app.
•	Uploads receiving QR image and basic donee details.
•	Generates app spending identity QR for each donee.
•	Adds and manages shopkeepers.
•	Reviews donor payment proofs.
•	Approves or rejects donation records.
•	Reviews shopkeeper spending records.
•	Views reports, audit logs, and risk flags.
5.2 Donor
•	Registers/logs in.
•	Views only approved donees.
•	Selects a donee.
•	Pays directly to the donee or approved receiver outside the app.
•	Uploads payment screenshot/reference.
•	Sees status as pending, verified, or rejected.
•	Views verified donation records in My Donation Records or Impact Wallet.
•	Receives spending visibility updates when shopkeeper records are approved.
5.3 Shopkeeper
•	Logs in through shopkeeper account created by admin.
•	Scans donee spending identity QR.
•	Records purchase amount, items, and optional receipt photo.
•	Sees own submitted spending records.
•	Does not receive settlement from the platform in MVP.
5.4 Donee
•	Has no app login.
•	Is found and verified offline.
•	Is added into the app only by admin.
•	May have a physical spending QR card.
•	May have a receiving QR linked to donee, guardian, or approved receiver.
6. QR Code Architecture
6.1 Receiving QR
The receiving QR is a real payment QR or payment/account detail image uploaded by admin. It is used by the donor to pay the donee or approved receiver directly outside the platform.
•	May be EasyPaisa, JazzCash, Raast, bank transfer QR, or another approved method.
•	May belong to the donee, guardian, family member, or approved receiver.
•	The platform only displays this QR.
•	The platform does not process the payment.
•	Donor submits screenshot/reference after paying.
6.2 Spending Identity QR
The spending identity QR is generated by the app. It is not a payment QR. It is used by shopkeeper to identify the donee and submit a purchase/spending record.
•	Example code: DONEE-SPEND-8FK29S
•	Route example: /shopkeeper/record-spending/DONEE-SPEND-8FK29S
•	Must not expose database ID.
•	Must not contain bank/payment credentials.
•	Can be printed as a physical card.
7. Product Flows
7.1 Offline Donee Verification Flow
15.	Team identifies a poor/deserving donee outside the app.
16.	Team verifies the case through field/community/reference checks.
17.	Team collects basic details and receiving QR from donee/guardian/approved receiver.
18.	Internal admin approves the donee for upload.
19.	Admin creates donee profile inside the app.
20.	Admin uploads receiving QR and generates spending identity QR.
21.	Donee becomes visible to donors only after approval.
7.2 Donor Direct Donation Flow
22.	Donor logs in.
23.	Donor browses approved donees.
24.	Donor selects a donee.
25.	App shows receiving QR and no-custody notice.
26.	Donor pays outside the app using EasyPaisa, JazzCash, Raast, bank, or other method.
27.	Donor returns to app and uploads screenshot/reference.
28.	Donation record status becomes Pending Verification.
29.	Admin verifies or rejects the proof.
30.	Verified record appears in donor’s My Donation Records/Impact Wallet.
7.3 Shopkeeper Spending Record Flow
31.	Donee visits approved shopkeeper with physical spending QR.
32.	Shopkeeper logs in and scans donee QR.
33.	App shows limited donee identity.
34.	Shopkeeper enters purchase amount and item details.
35.	Shopkeeper uploads optional receipt/photo.
36.	Spending record goes to admin review.
37.	Admin approves or rejects the spending record.
38.	If approved, donors who supported that donee receive visibility notification.

10. Database Design
10.1 Core Tables
Table	Purpose	Key Fields
profiles	Stores authenticated user profile and role.	id, full_name, phone, email, role, status, city
donors	Stores donor-specific totals for record reporting only.	profile_id, total_verified_donations, total_pending_donations, total_rejected_donations
shopkeepers	Stores approved shopkeeper details.	profile_id, shop_name, owner_name, phone, city, area, status
donees	Stores offline-verified donee details uploaded by admin.	full_name, guardian_name, city, area, need_category, receiving_method, receiving_qr_image_url, spending_qr_code, status
donation_records	Stores donor-submitted proof of direct external payment.	donor_id, donee_id, amount, payment_method, transaction_reference, proof_screenshot_url, status
spending_records	Stores shopkeeper purchase records for transparency.	shopkeeper_id, donee_id, amount, items_description, receipt_photo_url, review_status
notifications	Stores role-based alerts.	profile_id, title, message, type, metadata, is_read
audit_logs	Stores admin and financial-proof action history.	actor_profile_id, action, entity_type, entity_id, old_data, new_data
10.2 Critical Data Rule
•	Do not create wallet_balance.
•	Do not create topups.
•	Do not create internal transfer table.
•	Do not create shopkeeper receivable or settlement module in MVP.
•	Donation records are proof records only.
•	Spending records are transparency records only.
